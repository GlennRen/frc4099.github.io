History

Falcons FIRST was formed in the summer of 2011. Composed of 15 members, this nascent team was led by Poolesville senior Pavleen Thukral and Jack Zhu. Falcons FIRST worked tirelessly throughout the 2011-2012 school year to acquire funds, gather parts, and assemble a robot to compete in the 2012 FIRST season. Student programmers, builders, and public relations members worked hand in hand to put together a quality project and stimulate community interest in STEM. The team was able to raise over $21,000 in grants and donations and labored tirelessly to build the robot within an intense 6-week period. Their efforts would earn them the Rookie All-Star Award in the 2012 DC Regionals competition, in which they competed against 62 other schools in a robot basketball game, namely Rebound Rumble. Falcons FIRST also received significant recognition for its achievements in the Maryland Gazette and went on to compete in the 2012 World Championship in St. Louis.

This year, the team has expanded to over 60 members, many of whom were buoyed by the successes in last year's competition. Under the new leadership of Vineet Padia and Sidd Singal, Falcons FIRST has set its sights higher and aimed for victory in the national competition.

Mentors

Mr. Mark Curran

Mr. Mark Curran has been a vital and valuable mentor for the FIRST team 4099. His 40 years of teaching experience along with his engineering and mathematics background have been invaluable in the mentoring of team 4099 throughout the build season. In his own words Mr. Curran describes the mentoring process as, “a unique opportunity to solve a complex, electro engineering problem.” This was the second team that was under his leadership. His other accomplishments include bringing the Science, Mathematics and Computer Science Program, to the number one academically ranked school in Maryland (Poolesville HS (PHS)). Mr. Curran also instructs several rigorous engineering classes in PHS.
Mr. Mark Estep

Mr. Estep teaches computer science at Poolesville High School. He provides excellent instruction for freshmen and anyone who wishes to take his numerous classes in junior or senior year. He also offers many opportunities for his students to excel outside of the classroom. He and Mr. Stansbury have helped their students go to prestigious competitions such as DC3, CSAW, USACO and ACSL. His students have won at DC3 and CSAW while making high ranks in USACO and ACSL. Mr .Estep helps us greatly at FIRST Robotics by giving us supervision and excellent advice.

Poolesville High School

Poolesville High School, the only whole school magnet in Montgomery County, uniting close-knit, rural community values and diverse global perspectives, challenges its students to become confident, versatile, problem-solving citizens through rigorous cross-curricular pathways and meaningful, motivating learning experiences.

FIRST

FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) holds a series of competitions for kids of all ages. These competitions encourage students to combine science, technology, and their imagination to design and build robots to solve problems. It aims to foster teamwork and leadership.

About this Website

Development

This website was developed by the Falcons web team during the summer of 2012 using the Twitter Bootstrap framwork. It is being hosted on a Bluehost server. The blog is a Wordpress blog, and the forum is hosted by freeforums.org.

Updates

Website and blog updates are the responsibility of the Falcons public relations team. There are multiple writers working with the web team to keep the website as up to date as possible.

Future Plans

In the near future, the Falcons hope to convert their whole site to either a Drupal, Joomla, or Wordpress theme so that content management is made simpler and more efficient.
